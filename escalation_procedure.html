Tech Support Escalation Procedure

Steps to Escalate a Support Request

If you are unable to resolve an issue within the standard response time, or if an issue requires more specialized expertise, follow this escalation procedure to ensure it receives the appropriate attention:

  • Step 1: Gather Detailed Information

    Collect all relevant details about the issue, including screenshots, error messages, and user details. Ensure that the information is accurate and complete.

  • Step 2: Attempt Initial Troubleshooting

    Use standard troubleshooting procedures to attempt to resolve the issue. If no solution is found, proceed to the next step.

  • Step 3: Determine the Escalation Level

    Identify the urgency and complexity of the issue to determine the appropriate escalation level:

    • Level 1: Routine issue requiring assistance from a senior technician.
    • Level 2: Complex issue affecting multiple users or departments; requires intervention by a technical manager.
    • Level 3: Critical issue causing major disruptions; requires immediate action by the IT director or external vendor support.
  • Step 4: Document the Escalation

    Log the escalation in the support tracking system, including all relevant information and troubleshooting steps attempted. Mark the issue as “Escalated” with the chosen level.

  • Step 5: Notify the Escalation Team

    Contact the appropriate personnel based on the escalation level. Use email, chat, or a dedicated escalation hotline to ensure the issue is seen immediately. Clearly explain the urgency and provide all documented details.

  • Step 6: Monitor and Follow Up

    After escalation, monitor the status of the issue and follow up regularly to ensure it is being addressed. Keep the user informed of any updates or estimated resolution times.

  • Step 7: Close the Escalation

    Once the issue is resolved, document the solution in the support tracking system, and notify the user of the resolution. Close the ticket and update any relevant knowledge base articles if necessary.
